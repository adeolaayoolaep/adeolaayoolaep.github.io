UCLA · Biospecimen QC
Investigational Sample Chain-of-Custody & Audit Readiness
Problem

1,200+ biological samples lacked a standardized QC escalation pathway. Discrepancies reached analysis undocumented, creating direct risk to data integrity and participant safety.

Action

Established an acceptability assessment protocol for every incoming sample. Built chain-of-custody and audit-ready records integrated with clinical databases. Defined real-time escalation workflows to PIs. Implemented version-controlled SOPs across all procedures.

Result

Analytical errors reduced by more than 25% · Zero chain-of-custody breaks · Continuous audit-ready documentation

Duke · Multi-Site Audit
Multi-Site Feasibility Assessment & Data QA Infrastructure
Problem

A four-site human subjects study lacked formal QA infrastructure. Audit readiness was inconsistent across sites, data quality findings were addressed reactively, and feasibility decisions for new studies were made informally.

Action

Conducted formal feasibility assessments for all incoming protocols. Built data QA tools and trained junior team members on quality assurance and escalation pathways. Shifted audit preparation from reactive to continuous across 1,081 human samples.

Result

Zero data quality breaches across 3 years · Feasibility process formalized · Contributed to 2 NIH grant deliverables

LAUTECH · SOP Infrastructure
SOP Build-Out, Consent Training & Staff Compliance
Problem

Multi-site cohort studies on HIV, malaria, and Rotavirus had no SOPs, no formal consent training, no AE escalation pathway, and no standardized participant documentation across 10+ sites. Site operating at 30% capacity.

Action

Authored comprehensive IRB-aligned SOPs across all sites. Designed and delivered structured training for 50+ personnel covering informed consent (including low-literacy adaptations), chain-of-custody, AE identification, and regulatory documentation.

Result

30% to 100% site capacity in 6 months · 50+ staff trained · All 10+ sites audit-ready
